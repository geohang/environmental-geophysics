Introduction to Environmental Geophysics

Applied geophysics is the investigation of the Earth's crust and near-surface to achieve practical and economic aims. Unlike pure geophysics (global scale), we focus on the top 100 meters—where humanity interacts with the planet.

What is it?

The application of physics to investigate near-surface bio-physico-chemical phenomena. It differs from Engineering Geophysics (focused on structures/materials) but the two often overlap.

  • ➤ Engineering: "Can we build here?" (Voids, rock strength)
  • ➤ Environmental: "Is it polluted?" (Contaminants, landfills)
  • ➤ Forensic/Archaeo: "Is something hidden?" (Buried objects)

The Core Benefit

Traditional site investigation relies on drilling holes or digging pits ("Intrusive Methods"). This is expensive and slow.

"Instead of seeing geophysical survey as a cost, it should be viewed as adding value by making the entire site investigation more cost-effective."

— Reynolds (2011)

Survey Design: The Aliasing Trap

A common failure in survey design is Spatial Aliasing. This happens when the sampling interval is too large relative to the anomaly width. If you sample every 10m looking for a 2m pipe, you will likely miss it.

Handling Noise

Noise can obscure your target. Coherent noise (like power lines) is systematic. Incoherent noise (wind, traffic) is random.

About the site

A restored prairie for hands-on teaching and research

The Ashton Prairie Living Laboratory (APLL) is an 8-acre prairie restoration established in 2019 by the University of Iowa's School of Earth, Environment, and Sustainability. It sits within the Ashton Cross Country Course northwest of Iowa City, beside a small stream, and serves as a hands-on teaching and research site for ecology, hydrology, and the earth sciences.

The shallow subsurface beneath the prairie, its soils, weathering, and changing soil moisture, is exactly what near-surface geophysics is built to image. That makes APLL a convenient outdoor laboratory for this course: the same electrical, seismic, and electromagnetic methods taught in the modules were used here to characterize the ground.

The data on this page come from three field days in 2026. Electrical resistivity tomography (ERT) and seismic refraction lines were acquired on April 11, a GEM-2 frequency-domain EM survey (Profiles 01 to 09) on May 2, and further ERT on June 25. Every file is documented, quality-flagged, and released under CC BY 4.0, so you work with real acquisition geometry rather than idealized examples.

Seeing Water and Structure Underground

Environmental geophysics images the shallow subsurface with physics rather than a shovel. This course teaches the methods; the goal is to learn where water sits, how it moves, and how the weathered ground above bedrock, the critical zone, is put together.
Why geophysics

Four physical windows into the same ground

Each method senses a different physical property. No single one tells the whole story, so we combine them, then tie the result back to hydrology.

Density & magnetism

Gravity and magnetic surveys map mass and magnetic mineral contrasts: basins, bedrock, buried structure.

Seismic velocity

Refraction and surface waves track stiffness, which rises from loose soil to weathered rock to fresh bedrock.

Electrical properties

ERT, SP, and IP respond to pore fluids, clay, salinity, and interfaces. They are powerful but non-unique indicators of hydrologic conditions.

Electromagnetic fields

GPR, FDEM, TEM, and MT sense conductivity and dielectric contrasts from centimetres to kilometres deep.

The bridge

From resistivity to water content

Geophysics measures a physical property; hydrology wants water. Archie's law is one bridge between them and is implemented in PyHydroGeophysX. Move the sliders to isolate the modeled saturation effect.
